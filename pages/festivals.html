The Taste of Goan Celebrations

Festivals in Goa are incomplete without their signature sweets and snacks. From ancient temple fairs to exuberant carnivals, discover the symbolic foods that unite people in joyful celebration.

Jatra & Khaje

During temple fairs (Jatra), vendors sell crunchy, jaggery-coated Khaje—chickpea flour sticks tossed with sesame and ginger. A classic festival treat, Khaje is as much a memory as it is a flavor.

Hindu Festivals

Diwali & Boll

Boll (or Boli) are soft, sweet coconut-jaggery pancakes, steamed or griddled, and enjoyed during Diwali and Bhogi in Goan homes.

Diwali

Christmas & Dodol

Christmas brings Dodol—a rich, dark, sticky coconut-jaggery toffee, slow-cooked and sliced during the festive “Kuswar” sweet share.

Christmas

Feast & Sanna

Sannas, the pillowy fermented rice cakes, are a vital part of every Goan feast—be it Ganesh Chaturthi, church feasts, or Sunday Mass.

Universal

Ganesh Chaturthi & Neureos

These crescent-shaped pastries, filled with coconut, jaggery, and nuts, are made especially for Chovoth/Ganesh Chaturthi and Diwali.

Ganesh/Diwali

Gudi Padwa & Khatkhate

On Gudi Padwa, Goan families prepare Khatkhate—a fragrant vegetable stew with coconut, toor dal, jaggery, and teppal, marking the spring harvest and new beginnings.
