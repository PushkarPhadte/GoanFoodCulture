History & Origins

Ros Omelette originated as a quick and satisfying Goan street food for locals and tourists alike. It combines a simple omelette soaked in a spicy, tangy "ros" ‚Äî a Goan gravy made with tomatoes, spices, and tamarind or kokum. This dish reflects Goa's vibrant street culture and fusion heritage.

Authentic Ros Omelette Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Tamarind pulp or kokum, diluted (about 2 tbsp)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Beat the eggs well with some salt; set aside.
  2. Prepare the ‚Äúros‚Äù (gravy): Heat oil, saut√© onions until translucent. Add garlic and ginger pastes, fry for 1 min.
  3. Add tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder and salt. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  4. Add tamarind pulp or kokum water, bring to boil, simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Heat oil on a flat pan. Pour a thin layer of beaten eggs and make an omelette.
  6. Cut the omelette into slices or fold it, place on a serving plate and pour hot ros over it generously.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve immediately with bread or pav.
